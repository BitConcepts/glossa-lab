2. NOTE ON THE WRITING SYSTEM

As you noted, the corpus uses a syllabic (CV) writing system, not a consonantal abjad. Our current language model is built on Hebrew (an abjad), which means the phonotactic signal we are using is an imperfect match. This is acknowledged as a limitation: the Hebrew model captures consonantal skeleton patterns but cannot represent vowel sequences. The mapping inference is therefore approximate.

To address this properly, we are pursuing the Geez benchmark you suggested (see below).

---

The Geez benchmark is the critical validation: it is a fully deciphered syllabic system with ground truth.
