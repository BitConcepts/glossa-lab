## 1. Indus Research Centre / Roja Muthiah Research Library (RMRL)

- **Title**: Indus Research Centre — Tamil Nadu, India
- **Author/Editor**: RMRL / Indus Research Centre (hosted by Roja Muthiah Research Library, Chennai)
- **Year**: Ongoing (portal active as of 2026)
- **Publisher**: RMRL, Chennai
- **Type**: research tool / access portal
- **Access status**: web-accessible (IndusScript.in)
- **Reliability notes**: Institutional portal providing access to a Mahadevan-based corpus and concordance. States it provides "access to the corpus and concordance of the Indus script found at various Indus sites." Mahadevan's 1977 work is the baseline. Portal is a secondary access layer, not an independent primary source.
- **Contribution**: Quick lookup, concordance validation, evidence of active institutional support for the Mahadevan concordance.
- **Does NOT contribute**: Primary images, full excavation provenance, sign plates, or data beyond the Mahadevan M77 baseline.

## 3. Mahadevan, Iravatham (1977). *The Indus Script: Texts, Concordance and Tables*

- **Title**: The Indus Script: Texts, Concordance and Tables
- **Author/Editor**: Iravatham Mahadevan
- **Year**: 1977
- **Publisher**: Archaeological Survey of India / Deccan College, Pune
- **Type**: primary corpus / concordance
- **Access status**: partial — title page OCR only (raw text in data_raw/mahadevan_1977/). Full structured concordance not yet extracted. Indian Culture Portal and Archive.org may host accessible copies; manual acquisition needed.
- **Reliability notes**: Foundational work. Defines the M77 sign numbering system. Has known limitations in sign splitting/merging decisions that later work (Parpola, Fuls) refined. The baseline for most subsequent Indus research.
- **Contribution**: Baseline sign numbering (M001–M417+); concordance tables; inscription counts by site; early canonical dataset.
- **Does NOT contribute**: Photographs (sign plates are line drawings); spatial/temporal phasing; modern statistical treatments; expanded sign catalog beyond M77 scope.

## 4. Parpola, Asko; Pande, B.M.; Koskikallio, Petteri (eds.) (1979). *Corpus of Texts in the Indus Script*

- **Title**: Corpus of Texts in the Indus Script (Helsinki Annales Academiae Scientiarum Fennicae)
- **Author/Editor**: Parpola et al.
- **Year**: 1979
- **Publisher**: Suomalainen Tiedeakatemia (Finnish Academy of Science), Helsinki
- **Type**: primary corpus
- **Access status**: needs scan / institutional access — not currently in digital structured form locally. HELDa (University of Helsinki) may host a digitized version.
- **Reliability notes**: Foundational corpus compilation. Predates CISI by ~8 years. Uses Parpola sign numbering system (P-numbers). Covers inscription listings and early corpus baseline. Does not include the photographic quality of later CISI volumes.
- **Contribution**: Foundational corpus compilation; inscription listings; Parpola sign number definitions; historical corpus baseline.
- **Does NOT contribute**: High-resolution photographs; full site provenance; phasing; modern sign splitting detail.

## 5. Joshi, Jagat Pati; Parpola, Asko (eds.) (1987). *Corpus of Indus Seals and Inscriptions, Vol. 1: Collections in India*

- **Title**: Corpus of Indus Seals and Inscriptions, Vol. 1: Collections in India (CISI Vol.1)
- **Author/Editor**: Joshi, J.P.; Parpola, A. (eds.)
- **Year**: 1987
- **Publisher**: Suomalainen Tiedeakatemia, Helsinki (Annales Academiae Scientiarum Fennicae)
- **Type**: photo corpus / primary corpus
- **Access status**: partial — mayig/indus-valley-script-corpus (MIT license) provides a digitization of sign sequences from this source for a subset of ~179 Mohenjo-daro inscriptions. Full photographic plates not digitally available locally.
- **Reliability notes**: Primary photographic reference. High reliability for sign forms that are photographically documented. Sign sequences in the mayig digitization inherit CISI Vol.1 quality.
- **Contribution**: Image-backed sign validation; object-level verification; India collections (Mohenjo-daro subset); sign-form review.
- **Does NOT contribute**: Harappa data (that is in Vol.2); full site coverage; excavation phase data beyond object references.

## 15. Yadav, Nisha et al. (2010). "A Statistical Approach for Frequency Analysis of Indus Inscriptions"

- **Title**: A Statistical Approach for Frequency Analysis of Indus Inscriptions (PLOS ONE 4(12): e8305)
- **Author/Editor**: Yadav, N.; Kim, H.; Dwivedi, V.D.; Bhatt, N.; Sonawane, V.H.
- **Year**: 2010
- **Publisher**: PLOS ONE
- **Type**: secondary overview / statistical analysis
- **Access status**: available (open access, DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0008305)
- **Reliability notes**: Peer-reviewed statistical analysis. Provides Zipf-Mandelbrot parameters for the Indus corpus (α=1.00, β=2.74) and sign frequency tables. Good statistical baseline but derived from a specific CISI/ICIT dataset slice.
- **Contribution**: Frequency distribution parameters; sign frequency statistics; statistical analysis methodology.
- **Does NOT contribute**: Raw inscription data; images; new sign identifications.

## 16. Rao, Rajesh P.N. et al. (2009). "A Markov Model of the Indus Script"

- **Title**: A Markov Model of the Indus Script (PNAS 106(33): 13685-13690)
- **Author/Editor**: Rao, R.P.N.; Yadav, N.; Vahia, M.N.; Joglekar, H.; Adhikari, R.; Mahadevan, I.
- **Year**: 2009
- **Publisher**: PNAS
- **Type**: secondary overview / statistical analysis
- **Access status**: available (open access)
- **Reliability notes**: Peer-reviewed. Block entropy analysis supporting linguistic structure hypothesis. Conditional entropy sequence H1 > H2 > H3 (sub-linear growth consistent with natural language). Influential for establishing statistical baseline. Does not constitute decipherment.
- **Contribution**: Block entropy methodology; conditional entropy reference values; sign transition statistics.
- **Does NOT contribute**: Raw data; sign identifications; decipherment claims.

## 17. mayig/indus-valley-script-corpus (GitHub, MIT License, 2024-2025)

- **Title**: Indus Valley Script Corpus (mayig digitization)
- **Author/Editor**: mcskware (GitHub user)
- **Year**: 2024-2025
- **Publisher**: GitHub (https://github.com/mayig/indus-valley-script-corpus)
- **Type**: digital corpus digitization / derivative work
- **Access status**: available (MIT License; fully downloaded locally as indus_cisi_corpus.json)
- **Reliability notes**: Digitization of Parpola et al. CISI sign sequences in JSON format with Parpola P-number sign IDs. MIT license permits unrestricted use. Coverage: 179 inscriptions, all Mohenjo-daro (M-prefix). Feature vectors per sign are included (meaning uncertain). Reliability is contingent on the original CISI volumes; digitization introduces no new analysis — it transcribes sign IDs.
- **Contribution**: Machine-readable sign sequences with Parpola P-numbers; inscription IDs matching CISI Vol.1; artifact descriptions; feature vectors (purpose unclear without documentation).
- **Does NOT contribute**: Images; multi-site coverage (currently Mohenjo-daro only); damage markers; variant encoding; reading-direction metadata per inscription.
