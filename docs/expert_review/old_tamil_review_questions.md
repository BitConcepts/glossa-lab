# Review Questions for Dravidianist / Old Tamil Specialists

These questions are designed to solicit expert critique of the proposed
Proto-Dravidian readings for the Indus Script. We ask for honest assessment,
not endorsement.

---

## Phonological Plausibility

1. Are the proposed Proto-Dravidian reconstructed forms phonologically
   plausible for the period ~2600–1900 BCE? Which forms appear anachronistic?

2. The model assumes rebus-principle encoding (phonetic punning). Is this
   mechanism well-attested enough in early Dravidian to support 605 sign
   readings, or is it being over-applied?

3. Several readings use retroflex consonants (ḷ, ṟ, ṉ, ḻ) that we claim
   are Dravidian-exclusive. Is this phonological argument sound, or could
   these phonemes have existed in other language families of the region?

## DEDR Usage

4. Are the DEDR entries cited for each reading being used correctly? Are
   any entries being stretched beyond their documented semantic range?

5. For compound readings (e.g., M374=kul from DEDR 1709 kulam "clan"),
   are the semantic shifts from the DEDR entry to the proposed seal-context
   meaning reasonable?

6. Are any DEDR entries contested or known to have revised etymologies
   that would affect our readings?

## Tamil-Brahmi Concordance

7. The paper claims 58% concordance between IVS sign readings in
   terminal/name positions and attested Tamil-Brahmi personal name
   components (Mahadevan 2003). Is this comparison methodologically sound?

8. Given the ~1,500-year gap between IVC and Tamil-Brahmi, what degree
   of onomastic continuity is linguistically defensible?

9. Could the 58% concordance arise from chance overlap between a
   small phoneme inventory and common name components?

## Semantic and Cultural Interpretation

10. The model interprets most seals as guild-identity markers
    (professional titles + personal names). Is this consistent with what
    is known about Dravidian administrative vocabulary?

11. The tripartite grammar [CLASSIFIER – NAME – SUFFIX] is compared to
    later Arthaśāstra guild-superintendent titles. Is this comparison
    overreaching given the temporal gap?

12. Two signs (M374, M351) carry Munda substrate readings. Is the
    presence of Munda loanwords in a Dravidian administrative script
    linguistically plausible for the IVC period?

## Readings to Downgrade or Remove

13. Which specific readings in `dravidianist_anchor_subset.csv` should
    be downgraded from HIGH confidence or removed entirely?

14. Are there readings where the iconographic basis (sign shape →
    Dravidian word → phonetic reading) is unconvincing?

15. Are there alternative Dravidian etymologies for any signs that
    would fit better than our proposed readings?

## General

16. What would constitute a decisive test for or against the
    Proto-Dravidian reading framework?

17. Are there published Proto-Dravidian reconstructions (e.g.,
    Krishnamurti 2003) that contradict any of our readings?

18. What additional evidence would you need to see before considering
    any of these readings provisionally accepted?
