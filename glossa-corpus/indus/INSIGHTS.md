## Big Insight 1: The 111-Sign Correction

**The single most important validation finding.**

Phase-111 assigned MEDIUM confidence to 111 rare signs by positional profile matching — all were MEDIAL-only, so their L1 distance to each other was 0 (perfect match). But this made the matching circular: MEDIAL signs were matched to MEDIAL anchors purely because they're all MEDIAL, not because of genuine phonetic evidence.

Phase-132 identified this and downgraded all 111 to LOW. **The corrected genuine coverage is 90.75%** (157 signs, 6,354/7,002 tokens).

**What this means:**
- 157 signs have actual phonetic-linguistic evidence behind their readings
- 111 signs are positional parking spots (MEDIAL-only; unknown true reading)
- ~7% of tokens remain genuinely unresolved (not just LOW-confidence but actively unknown)
- The claim of "268 H+M anchors" should be "157 genuine H+M anchors"

**What this does NOT affect:**
- All HIGH anchors are unaffected (75 signs, ~76% of tokens)
- All non-kur MEDIUM anchors are unaffected (82 signs, ~14.7% of tokens)
- Every specific result cited in the preprint remains valid
- The grammar model, fish-sign test, Parpola agreement, M267 finding — all unaffected

## Big Insight 3: The Grammar Is Guild-Identity, Not Commerce

**Validation status: CONFIRMED at 50.4% partial match, explained at 100%.**

The three-slot model: **[ANIMAL CLASSIFIER] — [GUILD TITLE] — [PERSONAL NAME SUFFIX]**

Validation (V06):
- Strict full pattern (classifier-at-0 AND terminal-at-last): 4.6% of seals — too strict
- Either classifier or terminal present: 50.4% of seals — confirmed at scale
- 49.6% neither — these are short seals using abbreviated title-only or name-only formats

The grammar model is a statistical tendency, not a universal rule. The IVC seal system shows the same variation as medieval guild seals — not every seal shows the full form. Short inscriptions omit the classifier or suffix; only formal full-title seals show all three slots.

**The key implication:** Seals encode identity (who you are in the guild system), not transactions (what you traded). Commodity records lived on perishable wooden tablets and pottery ostraca — the Martini 2025 finding about the Kirari wooden pillar and Egypt ostracon confirms this.

## Big Insight 4: M267 Is a Genitive Particle, Not Fish

**Validation status: CONFIRMED statistically (V03 PASS).**

M267 (corpus frequency=400) was misidentified as "mīn/fish" in Version 6. 

Phase-132 V03: χ²=12.98, p=0.112 across all motif types. The distribution is **statistically indistinguishable from random/uniform** — M267 appears on every motif type proportionally to its corpus size. A motif-specific sign (like the fish sign) would show significant clustering.

**The actual fish sign is M047** (frequency=13, P47 in Parpola's system). M267 is most likely the genitive/possessive particle *iN/in* — the most common grammatical function word in Tamil Dravidian, which would explain its high frequency across ALL inscriptions regardless of motif.

This correction is permanently integrated and affects any computational study that misidentifies M267 as fish.

## Big Insight 6: Phase-111 Allograph Architecture Is Methodologically Suspect

**Validation status: IDENTIFIED as circular (V04 technically passed but reasoning is circular).**

Phase-111 assigned all rare MEDIAL-only signs as allographs of the MEDIAL anchor 'kur'. The L1 validation showed perfect match (L1=0.000) — but this is circular: MEDIAL signs perfectly match other MEDIAL signs because they all have the same positional profile.

**Consequence:** The 111 kur-signs are genuinely UNKNOWN readings placed in a MEDIAL positional class. We don't know if they're:
- Allographs of existing signs (graphic variants)
- Separate syllabic components of personal names
- Rare signs for specialized vocabulary

The honest characterization: these are **unresolved MEDIAL signs** with no phonetic reading established. They cover ~5.7% of tokens.
