# Manuscript Highlights
## Five bullet points for DSH structured submission

1. Non-random positional grammar confirmed: 0/2,000 permutations of within-seal sign shuffles exceeded the observed structure statistic (z = 10.3), establishing a three-slot INITIAL–MEDIAL–TERMINAL formula model across 97 distinct formula types.

2. Fish signs are never isolated: 0/140 fish-family sign occurrences across 9 corpus sites and the Gulf deposit catalog appear in isolation — the compound-only constraint is site-invariant, holding equally at the primary coastal port (Lothal) and inland sites.

3. Betweenness centrality stratifies grammar from name-syllable signs: in the full 161-sign directed bigram network, 20 signs have BC > 0 (grammar candidates) and 141 have BC = 0 — the network signature expected for a system encoding both grammatical structure and arbitrary personal-name phonetics.

4. M267 corrected: the sign previously identified as a fish/star pictograph (M267, frequency 400) is a high-frequency grammatical particle (81% MEDIAL, motif-independent by chi-square across all 9 sites), not an iconographic sign; the actual fish sign is M047 (frequency 13, 100% compound).

5. A complete public reproducibility package is provided: all 397 sign readings with confidence tiers, public data tables, validated analysis scripts, and a formal ICIT crosswalk and cross-corpus validation plan.
